# BasketGuard — Product Brief

## Working name

**BasketGuard**

The name is clear enough to keep for early development: it suggests protection of the user's grocery basket without overclaiming illegality or using language such as "price gouging" in the brand itself.

Alternative names held in reserve:

- BasketWatch
- PriceGuard
- BasketRinse
- ShelfWatch
- GroceryGuard
- PriceSpike

## Product thesis

UK supermarket shoppers do not need another basic grocery comparison app. They need a fast, evidence-based way to identify when a supermarket has disproportionately increased the price of products they actually buy.

BasketGuard should detect and report:

1. retailer-specific price spikes;
2. year-on-year price increases;
3. competitor price divergence;
4. shrinkflation;
5. weak loyalty-card promotions;
6. poor-value product outliers;
7. cheaper equivalent substitutions.

## One-sentence description

BasketGuard is a UK grocery price-behaviour tracker that flags when supermarkets raise prices disproportionately versus equivalent products at competitors.

## Example user-facing output

> Tesco warning: own-brand chopped tomatoes are up 55% year-on-year at Tesco, while equivalent products at Asda and Sainsbury's are broadly unchanged. Tesco is currently 31% above the cheapest comparable product. Switch retailer.

## Positioning

BasketGuard should not position itself as a legal authority on "price gouging". It should use evidence-based consumer language:

- price spike;
- poor-value outlier;
- retailer-specific increase;
- weak promotion;
- above-market increase;
- switch recommended.

## Core user promise

BasketGuard should save users time and money by turning grocery price history into simple, blunt recommendations.

Not:

> Here are 412 tomato products.

Instead:

> Tesco is poor value for these 7 things you buy.
